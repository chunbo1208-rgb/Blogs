Moonlit Night 月夜

Du Fu · 杜甫 Tang Poetry en 2026-03-27

Original: 今夜鄜州月，闺中只独看。
遥怜小儿女，未解忆长安。
香雾云鬟湿，清辉玉臂寒。
何时倚虚幌，双照泪痕干。

Tonight the moon over Fuzhou —

she watches it alone in her room.

Far off, I think of our small children

too young to understand longing for Chang'an.

Fragrant mist dampens her clouded hair;

cold moonlight chills her jade-white arms.

When will we lean against the empty curtains

and let the moonlight dry the tears on both our faces?

Notes

Du Fu (杜甫, 712–770 AD) wrote this during the An Lushan Rebellion, separated from his family in Fuzhou while trapped in occupied Chang'an. The poem is unusual in Tang poetry: rather than speaking in his own voice about missing home, Du Fu imagines his wife watching the same moon, grieving for him. The tenderness is devastating precisely because it is indirect.

The final image — moonlight drying tears — turns longing into hope. The reunion is not yet real, but the poem performs it: the same moon, the same light, briefly binding two separated people across a broken country.
