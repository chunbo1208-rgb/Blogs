Spring Dawn 春晓

Meng Haoran · 孟浩然 Tang Poetry en 2026-03-26

春眠不觉晓，

处处闻啼鸟。

夜来风雨声，

花落知多少。

Translation

Spring sleep, unaware of dawn —
everywhere the birds cry out.
Last night, the wind and rain —
how many blossoms fell?

Notes

Meng Haoran (孟浩然, 689–740 AD) was a close contemporary of Wang Wei and Li Bai. This jueju captures the hazy moment of waking in spring — the reluctance to leave sleep, the sudden awareness of birdsong, and the retrospective unease about last night's storm.

The final line — "how many blossoms fell?" — is one of the most iconic closings in Chinese poetry. It asks a question that can never be answered, transforming an ordinary morning into a meditation on impermanence.
