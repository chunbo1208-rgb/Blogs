静夜思

李白 唐诗 poem zh 2026-03-29

床前明月光，

疑是地上霜。

举头望明月，

低头思故乡。

English Translation

Before my bed, the bright moonlight
seems like frost upon the ground.
I raise my head to gaze upon the moon,
then lower it, and dream of home.

Notes

One of the most celebrated poems in the Tang Dynasty canon. Li Bai (701–762 AD) wrote this short jueju (絶句) — a four-line, five-character verse — while traveling far from his homeland. The poem derives its power from extreme economy: twenty characters, one image, one feeling.

The pairing of 明月 (bright moon) with 故乡 (hometown/homeland) is a classical Chinese trope — the moon as witness to separation, its cold light rhyming with frost, distance with downward gaze.
